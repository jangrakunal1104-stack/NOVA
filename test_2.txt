what is arp?

ASSISTANT:
ARP (Address Resolution Protocol) is a network protocol used for determining the MAC address of a device on a network, based on its IP address. It operates at Layer 2 (Data Link layer) of the OSI model and translates an IP address into a physical (MAC) address. This is necessary because while routers can forward data packets between different networks using IP addresses, they cannot directly communicate with devices on other networks without knowing their MAC addresses for sending frames within the local network.
